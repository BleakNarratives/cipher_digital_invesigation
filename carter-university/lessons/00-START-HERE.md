## What is Digital Forensics?

**Simple Answer**: It's detective work for computers and the internet.

**Longer Answer**: Digital forensics is the process of finding, preserving, and analyzing digital evidence. Think of it like being a detective, but instead of fingerprints and DNA, you're looking at computer files, blockchain transactions, and online activity.

## What is OSINT?

**OSINT** = Open Source Intelligence

**Simple Answer**: Finding information using publicly available sources.

**Example**: If someone posts their wallet address on Twitter, that's public information. You can look up that address on a blockchain explorer to see their transactions. That's OSINT!

## What is Cryptocurrency?

**Simple Answer**: Digital money that uses cryptography (secret codes) to work.

**Key Concepts**:
- **Wallet**: Like a bank account, but for crypto
- **Address**: Like an account number (public, anyone can see)
- **Private Key**: Like a password (secret, never share!)
- **Transaction**: Sending crypto from one wallet to another
- **Blockchain**: A public ledger that records all transactions
